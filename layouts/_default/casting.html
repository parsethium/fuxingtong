Casting

Our Precision Casting Division delivers advanced manufacturing solutions for high-performance components used in aerospace engines, industrial gas turbines, and other demanding applications. With state-of-the-art casting equipment and precise process control, we produce complex components with exceptional dimensional accuracy, mechanical performance, and reliability.

Advanced Manufacturing Infrastructure

Our casting and powder metallurgy operations enable the large-scale production of equiaxed, directionally solidified, and single-crystal components with consistent quality, which is supported by:

  • High-precision wax-press machines
  • Automated ceramic shell-making lines
  • Vacuum casting and directional solidification (DS/SX) furnaces
  • Advanced heat-treatment lines for solution, aging, and stress-relief processes
  • HIP processing units

Wide Range of Materials

  • Nickel-based superalloys for high-temperature component
  • Cobalt-based alloys for wear-resistant parts
  • High-strength stainless steels for structural and hot-section applications
  • Heat-resistant specialty alloys for power-generation equipment

Powder Metallurgy

We complement precision casting with Powder Metallurgy capabilities for small to medium-sized components.
This process expands material and design flexibility for critical aerospace and industrial applications.

  • Metal powders are compacted into the desired shapes and sintered to achieve full density
  • Allows production of complex geometries and materials difficult to cast
  • Post-processing heat treatment optimizes mechanical properties where required

Products & Applications

  • Turbine blades (equiaxed, DS, SX)
  • Vanes, nozzles, and stator components
  • Hot-section structural parts
  • Engine hardware and housings
  • Industrial gas-turbine parts
  • Components for aerospace and energy systems
Inspection Capabilities

Precision Casting Process

  • Wax Injection – forming high-precision wax patterns.
  • Assembly & Ceramic Shell Building – creating molds.
  • Dewaxing & Shell Firing – removing wax and strengthening mold.
  • Vacuum Casting (Equiaxed, DS, SX) – pouring superalloys.
  • Core Removal & HIP (if needed) – optimizing structural integrity.
  • Heat Treatment – solution, aging, and stress-relief cycles.

Powder Metallurgy Process

  • Powder Preparation & Compaction – forming powders using high pressure.
  • VSintering – heating components to densify microstructure.
  • Post-Processing Heat Treatment (if needed) – optimizing mechanical properties.
  • Optional HIP – eliminating porosity and improving structural integrity.
