Additive Manufacturing

Our Additive Manufacturing Division specializes in producing complex, high-performance components using state-of-the-art 3D printing technologies. We create rapid prototyping, low-volume production, and geometries that are impossible or costly to achieve with traditional manufacturing methods.

Additive Manufacturing Infrastructure

Our forming equipment allow production of components with intricate shapes, thin walls, and tight tolerances, meeting the demands of high-performance applications:

  • Metal Powder Bed Fusion (PBF/SLM/DMLS): high-precision production of small to medium components
  • Direct Energy Deposition (DED): large-scale metal parts and repair via hybrid fabrication
  • Wire-arc Additive Manufacturing (WAAM/DED): large-scale structural parts
  • Hybrid AM & Machining Cells: near-net-shape components ready for finishing
  • Automated powder handling and post-processing equipment
  • Integrated Heat Treatment and HIP Furnaces: stress relief, densification, and microstructure optimization

Wide Range of Materials

  • Nickel-based superalloys for high-temperature engine components.
  • Cobalt-based alloys for wear-resistant parts.
  • Titanium alloys for lightweight and high-strength castings.
  • Titanium alloys for aerospace and industrial components
  • Heat-resistant specialty alloys for gas turbines energy systems.

Key Benefits

  • Complex geometries not achievable with traditional methods
  • Rapid prototyping and production with shorter lead times
  • Material efficiency and weight reduction
  • Optimized mechanical properties through tailored micro structure and design
  • Low to medium volume production for specialized components
  • Flexible low-volume production for high-performance components
