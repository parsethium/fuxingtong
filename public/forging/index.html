Forging

Our Forging Division provides advanced manufacturing solutions for high-performance components used in aerospace engines, industrial gas turbines, and energy-generation systems. Combining state-of-the-art forging equipment, precise process control, and optimized heat-treatment systems, we produce components with superior mechanical properties, structural integrity, and dimensional accuracy.

Advanced Forging Infrastructure

Our infrastructure enables the production of a wide range of forged and ring-rolled components, consistently delivering superior metallurgical quality and mechanical performance. Our operations include:

  • Closed-die and impression-die forging presses for complex geometries
  • Precision hot-forging and cold-forging systems
  • Hydraulic and mechanical presses with high tonnage capacity
  • Ring rolling equipment for high-precision disks, rings, and large rotating components
  • Automated material handling and pre-heating furnaces
  • Advanced heat-treatment lines for solution, aging, and stress-relief processing

Wide Range of Materials

  • Nickel-based superalloys for high-temperature component
  • Cobalt-based alloys for wear-resistant parts
  • High-strength steels for structural and hot-section applications
  • Titanium alloys for specialized aerospace components
  • Heat-resistant specialty alloys for power-generation equipment

Products

  • Rotating Components: Shafts, Disks, Rotors, Forged Turbine, Compressor Blades
  • Ring-rolled Components: Air seals, Shrouds, Casings
  • Stationary Components: Cold-section Blades and Vanes, Stator Structures, Hot Section Engine Parts
Inspection Capabilities

Key Fabrication Steps

  • Material Preparation: Pre-heat high-performance alloys according to metallurgical specifications.
  • Hot/Cold Forging: Shape components in closed or impression dies to achieve desired geometry and grain flow.
  • Ring Rolling: Produce disks, rings, and large rotating components with precise dimensions.
  • Heat Treatment: Solution, aging, and stress-relief cycles to maximize mechanical properties.
  • Trimming & Preliminary Finishing: Remove flash and prepare components for downstream machining.
